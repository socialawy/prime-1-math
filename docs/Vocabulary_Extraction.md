# Vocabulary and instructional text:

- Extracted directly from the textbook to help ground the application's architecture:

### 1. Exact Vocabulary in Chapter 10 (Different Shapes)
The textbook introduces the following 3D and 2D shape terms exactly as written below:
* "Ball shape"
* "Cylinder shape"
* "Cube shape"
* "Prism shape"
* "Cuboid shape"
* "rectangle" (and plural "rectangles")
* "square" (and plural "squares")
* "circle" (and plural "circles")
* "triangle" (and plural "triangles")

*(Note: The book strictly appends the word "shape" after the 3D terms, e.g., "Cube shape," rather than just "Cube").*

### 2. Exact Instructional Text for the "Making 10" Strategy (Chapter 12)
When teaching $9 + 4$, the book uses the word "spliting" (spelled with one 't' in the text) and breaks the strategy into two methods. Here is the exact quote of the instructional logic:

**"► Make a 10 to add**
**How to add 9 + 4 ?**
**► You can add 9 + 4 by using two ways:**

**First Way: By spliting the smaller number**
1. 9 needs 1 more to make 10 so, split 4 into 1 and 3
2. Add 1 to 9 to make 10
3. 10 and 3 make 13

**Second Way: By spliting the greater number**
1. 4 needs 6 more to make 10 so, split 9 into 3 and 6
2. Add 6 to 4 to make 10
3. 3 and 10 make 13

**Notice:** You can split either number to make it easier for you to calculate."

### 3. "Ones" vs "Units" and "Sphere" vs "Ball"
* **"Units" vs "Ones":** The book uses **both**, but in strictly different contexts. 
    * "Units" is used for measurement (capacity and area). Its first occurrence is in Chapter 11 (How to Compare?): *"When water or any other liquid is in different containers, You can compare the amount by using "how many units"."*
    * "Ones" is used exclusively for place value. Its first occurrence is in the Table of Contents for Chapter 14: *"Ones, Tens, and Hundreds"* and *"Adding and subtracting ones"*.
* **"Sphere" vs "Ball":** The book strictly uses **"Ball"** and never uses the word "Sphere". The first occurrence is in Chapter 10, Lesson 10-1: *"Ball shape"*.
